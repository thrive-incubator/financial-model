Model limitations & open questions
No NPV / time value of money
All cash flows are shown in nominal dollars — $1M in year 10 is treated the same as $1M today. A proper investment case should discount future cash flows (e.g. at 8–10%) to get IRR and NPV. At a 10% discount rate, $10M in year 10 is worth ~$3.9M today.
OTC equity tracked but not modeled
The GU OTC equity slider appears in the UI but does not affect any calculation. It is informational only. If OTC co-invests or shares in returns, that needs to be wired into the equity and ROI calculations.
No correlation between revenue and exit probability
Every surviving venture has the same probability of reaching a liquidity event, regardless of revenue size. In reality, a $20M ARR company is far more likely to attract an acquirer than a $1M company. This understates equity returns for high-performing portfolios.
No post-maturity attrition
Survival rate is applied once at creation. Companies that survive never fail after that. In practice, ventures continue to fail at a lower rate even after reaching revenue maturity. This slightly overstates long-run royalty income.
IP ownership vs. licensing — open question
It is currently unclear whether Thrive retains IP ownership (with a license to the company) or transfers IP outright. This is a fundamental deal term: retained ownership creates leverage and ongoing royalty rights; a transfer may be cleaner for founders but weakens Thrive's long-term position.
Post-exit royalty continuity — assumed to stop
The model assumes royalties cease when a company is acquired or has a liquidity event. In practice, whether royalties survive a change of control is a critical contract term. Acquirers typically try to extinguish them; retaining them requires specific legal language.
Revenue-based vs. profit-based royalties
Royalties are currently calculated on gross revenue. This can penalize early-stage companies with high costs. An alternative is to base royalties on gross margin or EBITDA once revenue crosses a threshold. The right choice depends on the type of company and the stage at which royalties kick in.
Advanced deal terms not modeled
Several common incubator mechanisms are excluded from this model: royalty buyout (company pays a lump sum to exit the royalty obligation); royalty-to-equity conversion (accumulated royalties convert to additional equity at a milestone); pro-rata rights (Thrive's right to invest in future rounds to maintain its equity stake). These terms can significantly change returns and should be explored in later-stage term sheet design.
